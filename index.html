Surah Baqarah Last 2 Ayat - Introduction, Benefits, and Translation

Introduction

Surah Baqarah last 2 ayats are some of the Quran's most important and spiritually significant ayats. These 2 ayats are located in the second surah of the Quran, Al-Baqarah(The Cow), starting from the ayat number 285 to 286, they are rich with meaning, guidance, and comfort for believers. The surah tells us the history of the previous ummahs, their attitude towards Islam, and their messengers.

The ayats highlight the importance of faith in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, and the obedience of believers. These are the core principles of Islam that a person who claims to be a Muslim must adhere to. Memorization and recitation of these 2 ayats are highly encouraged due to their significance and are said to offer defense against whispering, witchcraft, and evil eyes.

The Revelation of the Surah Baqarah Last 2 Ayats

The revelation of the last two ayats of Surah Baqarah has a unique story, as the Muslims are told and fascinated about the significant moment known as the Night Journey(Isra' and Mi'raj). It was a miraculous event, the Prophet (ﷺ) was taken from Mecca to Jerusalem and ascended to the heavens. Throughout the whole journey, the Prophet (ﷺ) was given three gifts from Allah, those are:

  • The five daily prayers, an obligation on the believer(at a certain age).
  • The last two ayats of Surah Al-Baqarah.
  • It was promised to him (ﷺ) that the sins of those who do not associate anything with Allah would be forgiven.

Unlike most of the Quran that was sent down at Muhammad (ﷺ) on Earth, the last two ayats of Surah Baqarah were revealed to Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) from under the throne of Allah, from the divine treasure while traveling towards the Heavens. These two ayats were written by Allah on Loh-e-Mahfooz, 2000 years before he even created the Heavens and the Earth.

Significance of the Last 2 Ayats

  • The significance of the last 2 ayats of Surah Baqarah is reported in various hadiths, in Sahih Muslim, hadith no.780, it is narrated by Abu Huraira (RA), The Messenger (ﷺ) of Allah said: 'Do not turn your houses into graves. Indeed, Shaytan does not enter the house where Surah Baqarah is recited.

Although this hadith is about the entire Surah Al-Baqarah, the last two verses are often singled out for their particular power in warding off evil and protecting from harmful forces.

  • According to Sahih Muslim Book 04, hadith no.1760, Ibn Abbas narrated that the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) was sitting with Jibrael, a gate of Heaven opened that was never opened before and an angel descended through it who never came down to the earth before. The angel greeted and said, Rejoice in two lights given to you which have not been given to any prophet before youOne of the lights was the last 2 ayats or the concluding ayats of Surah Baqarah.

The recitation of these ayats is a self-reminder of the Islamic Core beliefs that are also the key pillar of faith and a way to asses one's attitude compared to the believers described in the last ayat 285. The ayat 286 serves as a supplication for asking Allah's mercy, forgiveness, and relief from burdens.

Surah Baqarah Last 2 Ayats Benefits

Aside from good deeds one gets on reciting the Al-Quran, Surah Baqarah's last 2 Ayats are recited for other purposes. The meaning of the ayats is also of a dua, there are several reported hadiths of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) encouraging the recitation of the last 2 ayats of Surah Baqarah for various purposes and situations.

Protection from Harm and Evil:

  • Reciting the last two verses of Surah Baqarah acts as a shield against harm, Shaytan (Satan), and other evil powers. The Prophet (ﷺ) highlighted the protective power of these ayats in multiple hadiths.
Reference:

"Shaytan does not enter a house in which Surah Al-Baqarah is recited." (Sahih Muslim, Hadith 780)

By reciting these ayats, believers can guard their homes and families from the negative influence of evil and satanic forces, which ensures spiritual protection and peace.

Sufficiency and Security:

  • The last two ayats are enough for a believer in terms of protection, comfort, and security. To fulfill the needs of those who recite them. The Prophet (ﷺ) encouraged us to recite them, especially at night.
Reference:

"Whoever recites the last two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah at night, they will suffice him." (Sahih Bukhari, Hadith 5009; Sahih Muslim, Hadith 807)

This applies to those who recite the last two ayats before sleeping. However, there are two interpretations of this hadith, the first one is that the recitation of these ayats will suffice for any need of other azkar and night prayer, and the other one is that it is sufficient to protect the believer from evil.

Forgiveness of Sins:

The last 2 ayats of Surah Baqarah are a source of divine forgiveness and mercy. The translation of the second ayat contains a beautiful dua for asking forgiveness from Allah.

  • Our Lord! Do not punish us if we forget or make a mistake. Our Lord! Do not place a burden on us like the one you placed on those before us. Our Lord! Do not burden us with what we cannot bear. Pardon us, forgive us, and have mercy on us. You are our ˹only˺ Guardian. So grant us victory over the disbelieving people. (Al-Quran, Surah Baqarah, Ayat 286)

The recitation of these ayats with sincerity is a means of asking for and obtaining Allah's forgiveness, especially for unintentional mistakes or sins that are overlooked.

Strengthening of Faith:

The most important and powerful aspect of reciting and pondering the Al-Quran is strengthening one's faith. The last 2 ayats affirm the believer's commitment to Allah, His messengers, and His divine revelation. These verses also reaffirm the Muslim faith in the unseen and guidance from Allah. These ayats are a strong reminder of Islamic core beliefs.

Relief from Hardship and Burdens:

One of the key benefits of reciting the last 2 ayats of Surah Baqarah is the relief from hardships and burdens and a source of comfort. While reciting the believers ask Allah not to place such a burden on them that they can not bear. By making this dua, Muslims seek Allah's assistance in overcoming challenges and trust that He will not test them beyond their capacity.

Surah Baqarah Last 2 Ayats in English

Quran was revealed in Arabic and is spoken in the Middle East, North Africa, and some areas of the Horn of Africa. Although the Quran's Arabic is not straightforward, the richness and depth of the language often make it challenging to convey the full spectrum of its meanings in English. So, to understand the meaning of Surah Baqarah's last 2 ayats, one of the most common English translations and a summarized version of Tafseer Ibn Kaseer is added below.

Arabic:

آمَنَ الرَّسُولُ بِمَا أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ رَبِّهِ وَالْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍ مِنْ رُسُلِهِ ۚ وَقَالُوا سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ الْمَصِيرُ (2:285)

Translation:

The Messenger believes in what has been sent down to him from his Lord, and (so do) the believers. Each one believes in Allah, His Angels, His Books, and His Messengers. (They say,) "We make no distinction between one another of His Messengers, (and they say) We hear, and we obey. (We seek) Your forgiveness, our Lord, and to You is the return (of all)." Al-Quran(2:285)

Tafseer:

In the first ayat, Allah emphasizes the core beliefs of the believers in Islam. All the believers believe Allah is the One and Only, and the Sustainer. No one is worthy of worship except Him and no Lord except Him. They proclaim their faith in Allah, His angels, His books, and His messengers without distinguishing between them such as believing in some of them and rejecting others.

They also believe in all prophets and messengers sent by Allah, considering them truthful and righteous. Although some prophets introduced laws that replaced previous ones by Allah's will, the final law given to Muhammad (ﷺ), the last prophet, abrogated all earlier laws. Muhammad's (ﷺ) law will remain valid until the Day of Judgment, with a group of his followers always steadfast in the truth.

The last part of this ayat reflects the attitude of submission and obedience to Allah. The believers acknowledge Allah's commandments, they understand and act upon them. They seek forgiveness for their deeds and rely on the mercy and kindness of Allah. They acknowledge the fact that everyone will ultimately return to Allah.

Arabic:

لَا يُكَلِّفُ اللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِلَّا وُسْعَهَا ۚ لَهَا مَا كَسَبَتْ وَعَلَيْهَا مَا اكْتَسَبَتْ ۗ رَبَّنَا لَا تُؤَاخِذْنَا إِنْ نَسِينَا أَوْ أَخْطَأْنَا ۚ رَبَّنَا وَلَا تَحْمِلْ عَلَيْنَا إِصْرًا كَمَا حَمَلْتَهُ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِنَا ۚ رَبَّنَا وَلَا تُحَمِّلْنَا مَا لَا طَاقَةَ لَنَا بِهِ ۖ وَاعْفُ عَنَّا وَاغْفِرْ لَنَا وَارْحَمْنَا ۚ أَنْتَ مَوْلَانَا فَانْصُرْنَا عَلَى الْقَوْمِ الْكَافِرِينَ (2:286)

Translation:

Allah burdens not a person beyond his scope. He gets reward for that (good) he has earned, and he is punished for that (evil) he has earned. "Our Lord! Punish us not if we forget or fall into error, our Lord! Lay not on us a burden like that which You did lay on those before us (Jews and Christians); our Lord! Put not on us a burden greater than we have strength to bear. Pardon us and grant us forgiveness. Have mercy on us. You are our Mawla (Patron, Supporter, and Protector) and give us victory over the disbelieving people." Al-Quran(2:286)

Tafseer:

The last ayat of the second ayat of our discussion reminds us of Allah's absolute kindness, justice, forgiveness, blessing, and mercy. The ayat reassures believers that they will not be tested or asked for a burden they can not handle. This shows that although Allah will question his servants and judge them, He will only punish for what one can protect himself.

As for what one can not protect himself from, the servant is exempted from the punishment. He gets a reward for that which he has earned, of good, and gets punished for that he has earned, of evil, and as we discussed earlier, concerning the acts he is responsible for. Furthermore, the believer seeks forgiveness from his lord for the acts for which he might have forgotten the obligation(such as prayer) or fallen into sin without knowing.

The act of making dua from the calamities that befall the early people, meaning the Christians and Jews. The believer asks Allah not to reveal his sins or expose them that is between him and his Lord. In the last dialogue, the believer asks for Allah's mercy as he is the supporter, sustainer, and helper. For we know to Allah we all shall return, and we ask Him to give us victory over the disbelieving people.
